HOW TO PLAY SPADES

Overview

Spades is a 4-player trick-taking card game played in teams. You (South) partner with North against West and East. First team to 500 points wins.

Bidding

  • Each player bids how many tricks they expect to win (1–7), or bids Nil (0).
  • Your team’s combined bid is the contract you must meet.
  • Nil = you promise to take zero tricks. +100 if successful, −100 if you fail.

Playing Tricks

  • The lead player plays any card (except Spades until broken).
  • Other players must follow the led suit if they can.
  • If you can’t follow suit, you may play any card including Spades (trump).
  • Highest card of the led suit wins — unless a Spade was played, then highest Spade wins.

Breaking Spades

Spades cannot be led until a Spade has been played on a previous trick (trumping). Once broken, Spades can be led freely.

Scoring

  • Meet your team bid: 10 points per bid trick + 1 point per overtrick (bag).
  • Fail your bid: −10 points per bid trick.
  • Accumulate 10 bags: −100 point penalty.
  • Nil success: +100 points. Nil fail: −100 points.

Entry & Payout

  • Win payout: 125 credits
